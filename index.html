👉 About Me:-

I am Pritam Mitra, a Ph.D. Research Scholar and UGC-Junior Research Fellow at the Department of Geography, Central University of Punjab, India. My research lies at the intersection of Geoinformatics, Remote Sensing, GIS, and Environmental Systems Science, with a strong focus on understanding complex interactions between hydro-meteorological hazards, urban environments, and socio-economic vulnerability. My academic journey has been driven by a deep interest in spatial thinking and data-driven environmental analysis, particularly in the domains of flood risk assessment, urban thermal dynamics, climate variability, and geohazard monitoring. I extensively work with multi-source geospatial datasets, including Landsat, Sentinel, SAR, IMD climate records, and CMIP6 projections, to model and interpret environmental change at regional scales. In addition to traditional geospatial methods, my research integrates advanced spatial statistics and machine learning techniques such as Logistic Regression, Frequency Ratio, Weight of Evidence, and ensemble learning models including Random Forest, SVM, and Boosting algorithms. This integration helps in developing more reliable and interpretable hazard susceptibility and environmental risk models. Beyond technical analysis, I am deeply interested in social vulnerability and inequality studies, aiming to bridge the gap between physical environmental processes and human systems. My work contributes toward disaster risk reduction, sustainable urban planning, and climate-resilient development strategies. I am also actively engaged in academic writing, conference presentations, and collaborative research, continuously striving to contribute meaningful insights to the field of Earth System Science and Geospatial Intelligence.
